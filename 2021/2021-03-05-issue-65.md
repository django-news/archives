## News

### Happy Anniversary to Python and the Python Software Foundation!

<p>This week Python turned 30, and the Python Software Foundation turned 20 years old. In celebration, the PSF has a membership drive with the goal of getting 2,000 new or renewing members over the next 30 days. If you aren't a basic member, there is both a free and $99/year option to support the foundation.</p>

[https://pyfound.blogspot.com/2021/03/happy-anniversary-to-python-and-python.html](https://pyfound.blogspot.com/2021/03/happy-anniversary-to-python-and-python.html)

### Python Developers Survey 2020 Results

<p>JetBrains has released its annual Python developer survey which is full of graphics and interesting info on how the community uses Python in practice.</p>

[https://www.jetbrains.com/lp/python-developers-survey-2020/](https://www.jetbrains.com/lp/python-developers-survey-2020/)

### Wagtail CMS: Accessibility team reloaded

<p>After 6 months of operations, we reflect on the progress our accessibility team has made and go on the lookout for new contributors.</p>

[https://wagtail.org/blog/accessibility-team-reloaded/](https://wagtail.org/blog/accessibility-team-reloaded/)

## Django Articles

### DjangoCon Europe 2021 CFP is open

<p>The Call for Proposals is open for DjangoCon Europe 2021 and are due by April 1.</p>

[https://2021.djangocon.eu/talks/cfp/](https://2021.djangocon.eu/talks/cfp/)

### PyCon AU 2021

<p>Following the success of PyConline AU 2021, and with the lack of certainty in the world, PyCon AU is again running virtually in 2021 as PyConline AU, in UTC+10 (Australian Eastern Standard Time). We intend to run in late Q3 (~ September), but we have yet to confirm the exact date.</p>

[https://2021.pycon.org.au/](https://2021.pycon.org.au/)
